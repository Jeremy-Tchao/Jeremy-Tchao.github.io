ZHAOHUI ZHAO

KIDSWARE

This project is a concept proposal of a system, that aims to address the issue of the waste of child clothes, toys and supplies: as children grow, the objects in question will not and therefore will become unfit to children. The system proposed employs a network of specially designed trade stations that sterilizes, repackages and thereby the item for immediate resell.

Product Concept:
To build a sustainable system in order to provide a series of services for parents to have a platform to exchange no-longer-fit child clothing and other supplies with each other in local communities

Research

  • The waste of used child clothing and supply poses economical burdens and environmental concerns, average American households spend $528 dollars per year on child care supplies.
  • Parents have concerns for the quality of purchased children supplies varies: are they going to be harmful to the children? But those worn by other children is proven to be of good quality, otherwise they would not have been kept until they are no longer fit, so we can indirectly do the gatekeeping for the parents.
  • The time concerns and hassles for trading online: most of the time it calls for packaging and mailing, takes efforts and have to wait for days, but a locally located station offers instant pick-up or drop-off.

Design Goals and Concept
Create an app and suitable hardware station for the exchange and enable the users to interact with the platform with ease.

After we were finished with the high-level brainstorming and industrial design, I then shifted to designing the interface for both the mobile app and the station’s panel.

I started with a tree diagram: first I listed what functions needs to be made available to the user, then I organized them into interfaces that needs to be implemented and connected the interfaces in paths to form the tree diagram.

Then I started to determine some key design elements to be applied throughout the design: the first was the color, I went with a shade of blue that creates intimacy and a sense of relaxation to hint user of a mood for trading child care items; then I decided to avoid sharp edges for the same reasons stated, and use rounded rectangles with consistent radius of curvature as the key element.

After settling on the high-level design factors, I then proceeded to design the individual interfaces for the mobile app and the station.

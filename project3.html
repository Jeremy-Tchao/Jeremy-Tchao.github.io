ZHAOHUI ZHAO

KIDSWARE INTERFACE

This project is a concept proposal of a system, that aims to address the issue of the waste of child clothes, toys and supplies: as children grow, the objects in question will not and therefore will become unfit to children. The system proposed employs a network of specially designed trade stations that sterilizes, repackages and thereby the item for immediate resell.

My role in this project was first brainstorming with my partner on how the product should work in a high level.

After we are finished with the high-level brainstorming and industrial design, I then shifted to designing the interface for both the mobile app and the station’s panel.

I started with a tree diagram: first I listed what functions needs to be made available to the user, then I organized them into a interfaces that needs to be implemented and connected the interfaces in paths to form the tree diagram.

Then I started to determine some key design elements to be applied throughout the design: the first was the color, I went with an shade of blue that creates intimacy and a sense of relaxation to hint user of a mood for trading childcare items; then I decided to avoid sharp edges for the same reasons stated, and use rounded rectangles with consistent radius of curvature as the key element.

After settling on the high-level design factors, I then proceeded to design the individual interfaces.

And the feature I feel that would do the most to help the user is the horizontal-scrolling lists on the home page, as it packs high density of information with ease of navigating and without taking up the whole screen.

TYPOGRAPHY FORM EXPERIMENT

In this project, I am tasked with creating an "intriguing" composition using Helvetica.

Thinking of Helvetica, the first thing that popped into my mind is "boring." As it has been used so wildly to the extent that people became numb to the typeface -- It is THE typeface. So the notion of creating a "intriguing" composition using Helvetica is rather challenging for me at first.

But recalling the time when Helvetica was first introduced, which was 1957, the fact that this typeface is almost 60 years old yet is still modern and functional enough to be used in almost everywhere and in every forms of content is mind-blowing: from the largest printed poster to the smallest digital screen, from the government document to the poster of art films.

This longevity is no coincidence: it is the orderly, minimalistic design that made Helvetica a classic. And I wanted to illustrate that design feature of Helvetica using its characteristic straight and steadfast strokes.

The result is this almost futuristic composition: I hand-picked the letters used -- NEXT. First, the word "next" itself gives out a futuristic atmosphere already, and the sharps, decisive and clear strokes in the letters created a minimalistic look that represents the vision for the future.
